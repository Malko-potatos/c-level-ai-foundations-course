# Product

## Register

product

## Users

This survey is for Korean C-level leaders and software developers who are considering an AI foundations course. They often open the page on mobile from a chat message, email, or social link, then answer quickly between meetings or work sessions. They need to express their current AI literacy, learning goals, and priority keywords without feeling tested.

## Product Purpose

The product gathers demand signals for an AI foundations lecture series. Success means respondents can finish in about three minutes, submit reliable answers, and help the course owner tune the curriculum for both executive and developer audiences.

## Brand Personality

Confident, concise, and premium. The interface should feel like a focused editorial intake form rather than a generic SaaS survey. It should communicate that the course will be practical, strategic, and thoughtfully curated.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI landing pages with purple gradients, neon dark dashboards, over-explained cards, stock robot imagery, and decorative animation that slows down form completion. Avoid survey tools that feel bureaucratic or exam-like.

## Design Principles

1. Make progress visible so respondents always know what remains.
2. Keep the task lightweight, but make the output feel valuable.
3. Separate executive strategy and developer implementation without splitting the experience.
4. Use the Airbnb-inspired visual system sparingly: soft surfaces, strong type, one Rausch primary action.
5. Motion should clarify state changes, not perform for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, 44px minimum touch targets, reduced-motion support, and clear Korean UX copy. The form should remain usable when motion is disabled and when users navigate with a keyboard or screen reader.
